Xanthos And Letoon (Official Unesco Site)

Located about 200 km west of Antalya, these twin sites were the center of the Lycian civilization.

Xanthos: The capital of ancient Lycia, famous for its unique funerary art and pillar-mounted tombs.

Letoon: The spiritual sanctuary of the Lycian League, dedicated to Leto and her twin children, Artemis and Apollo.

Hierapolis-Pamukkale (Official Unesco Site)

Often visited as a day trip from Antalya (~240 km), this "Cotton Castle" is a dual-wonder.

Hierapolis: An ancient Roman spa city built atop the white travertine terraces. Highlights include the Frontinus Gate and one of the largest ancient Necropolises (cemeteries) in Turkey.

The Antique Pool: You can swim in mineral-rich waters among original Roman columns that fell during an earthquake.

Antalya is often called the "Turkish Riviera," but beyond its luxury resorts lies a landscape densely packed with ancient civilizations. The region was a crossroads for the Hittites, Greeks, Romans, Seljuks, and Ottomans, leaving behind some of the best-preserved ruins in the Mediterranean.
